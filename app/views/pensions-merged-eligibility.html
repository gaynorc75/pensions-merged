Who gets the basic State Pension

You get the basic State Pension if you’re:

  • a man born before 6 April 1951
  • a woman born before 6 April 1951

If you were born later, you'll get the new State Pension instead.

This guide is also available in Welsh (Cymraeg).

Your National Insurance record

To get the basic State Pension you must have enough qualifying years on your National Insurance record.

A qualifying year is a tax year (April to April) during which you:

  • worked and paid National Insurance
  • got National Insurance Credits
  • paid voluntary National Insurance contributions

The number of qualifying years of National Insurance contributions you have, affects the amount of State Pension you will get.

Number of qualifying years needed
Gender and year of birth Qualifying years needed
Men born before 1945 11
Women born before 1950 10
Men born between 1945 and 1951 1
Women born between 1950 and 1953 1

If you're married or in a civil partnership

You might be able to increase or inherit State Pension if you have had a spouse or civil partner and you're either:

  • not eligible for the basic State Pension
  • getting less than £141.85 per week

If you're transgender

Your State Pension might be affected if you’re a transgender person and you:

  • were born between 24 December 1919 and 3 April 1945
  • have claimed State Pension before 4 April 2005
  • can provide evidence that your gender reassignment surgery took place before 4 April 2005

Find out more and contact the Gender Recognition team

You do not need to do anything if you legally changed your gender and started claiming State Pension on or after 4 April 2005 - you'll already be claiming based on your legal gender.
