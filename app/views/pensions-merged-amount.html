Checking your amount

Your State Pension forecast will tell you how much you can get and when.

There are two types of state pension depending on when you were born.

You'll get new State pension if you're:

  • a man born on or after 6 April 1951
  • a woman born on or after 6 April 1953

You'll get basic State Pension if you were born before these dates.

How it's calculated

Your final amount is based on a number of different things.

Your National Insurance record

You’ll usually need to have 10 qualifying years on your National Insurance record to get any new State Pension. They do not have to be 10 years in a row.

Check your National Insurance record to see how many qualifying years you have.

Your pension amount will increase as you add qualifying years. You will need 30-35 qualifying years to get the full amount of State Pension depending on when you reach state pension age.

A qualifying year applies if:

  • you were working and paying National Insurance contributions
  • you were getting National Insurance credit, for example if you were unemployed, ill, or a parent or carer
  • you were paying voluntary National Insurance contributions

If you were contracted out of Additional State Pension

Before 2016, some people paid into an Additional State Pension. However, your employer might have chosen to pay more into your workplace pension and less into your State Pension. This means you were ‘contracted out’ of the Additional State Pension - you would have paid less National Insurance.

Your State Pension might be lower if you’ve ever been contracted out from the Additional State Pension.

If you were contracted out of the Additional State Pension (also known as State Second Pension or ‘SERPs’) your National Insurance contributions were either:

  • lower than people paying into the Additional State Pension
  • paid into another pension, for example a private pension

Check if you’ve been contracted out.
